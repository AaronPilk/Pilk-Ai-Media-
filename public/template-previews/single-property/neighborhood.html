The Setting

An island built for the few who know it.

Carved from Tampa Bay in the 1920s, Davis Islands is one of the city's most coveted addresses — a pair of man-made islands wrapped in protected coves, mature oaks, and Mediterranean-revival architecture. It carries the rare combination of true waterfront living and a genuine neighborhood: people walk to coffee, wave from the docks, and know their barista by name.

Despite sitting minutes from downtown Tampa and Water Street, the island feels removed and quiet — its own village center, yacht club, and private airfield keeping life contained and unhurried. It is the kind of place residents rarely leave once they arrive.

On the Water

Boating & the Bay

Direct, no-bridge access to Tampa Bay and the Gulf. Sandbars, waterfront dining by boat, and sunset cruises are a daily option from your own dock.

The Village

Davis Islands Village

A walkable cluster of cafés, a wine bar, boutique fitness, and the island market — the social center of the neighborhood, all minutes from the residence.

Outdoors

Parks & Trails

Marjorie Park, the dog beach, the seaplane basin promenade, and tennis courts wrap the islands in green space and waterfront walking paths.

Members' Club

Davis Islands Yacht Club

One of Florida's oldest yacht clubs sits three minutes away — racing, dining, and a calendar of member events at the water's edge.

Dining

Tampa's Best, Nearby

From island cafés to the award-winning kitchens of Hyde Park, Water Street, and Armature Works — all within a short drive or boat ride.

Culture

Downtown & the Riverwalk

The Tampa Riverwalk, Sparkman Wharf, the Straz arts center, and pro sports are six minutes away — city energy without city living.

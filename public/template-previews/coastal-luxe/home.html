About this home

Tucked onto a quiet point with open Gulf water on three sides, this is the kind of place you slow down in. Mornings start on the dock with coffee and dolphins in the channel; evenings end by the pool watching the sky turn. Inside, the layout is easy and bright — high ceilings, walls of glass, and a kitchen built to spill out onto the covered lanai. It's a real coastal home, not a showpiece: comfortable, well-kept, and ready for the next chapter.

Dock & beach access

The dock is the whole point. Deep, protected water means you keep the boat in the back yard year-round — no trailer, no marina fees, no waiting list. You're a five-minute idle to open Gulf and ten minutes by water to the sandbars off Shell Key. On foot, the sugar-sand beach is a four-block walk down a quiet residential lane, with a deeded neighborhood access point reserved for residents.

The neighborhood

Pelican Point sits on the bay side of Treasure Island, one of the most tightly held pockets on the barrier islands. It's a real neighborhood — kids on bikes, neighbors who wave, golf carts to the beach — wrapped around some of the best boating water on the central Gulf coast. You're walking distance to John's Pass village, a short drive to St. Pete Beach and downtown St. Petersburg, and forty minutes to Tampa International.

Lifestyle

Island life, the easy way.

Treasure Island is barefoot living done right — wide soft-sand beaches, a walkable strip of cafés and ice cream shops, and some of the best sunsets on the Gulf. You're ten minutes from St. Pete Beach and a short hop to downtown when you want the city. From this dock, the water is yours by sunrise.

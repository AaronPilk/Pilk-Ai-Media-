The development

A tower built for downtown.

LOFT52 is a 52-story residential tower on Central Avenue — the tallest address in downtown St. Petersburg, and the first to put the whole city within a ten-minute walk of your front door.

The story

Why we built it.

St. Petersburg outgrew its skyline. The waterfront, the arts district, the restaurants, the new pier — all of it arrived faster than the housing did. LOFT52 was conceived to close that gap: a single, design-led tower that puts residents in the center of everything, without asking them to compromise on light, space, or service.

Every residence is a loft at heart — high ceilings, exposed structure, and floor-to-ceiling glass — refined with the finishes and amenities you'd expect from a flagship address. The result is a building that feels like the city itself: open, modern, and unapologetically urban.

Design philosophy

Built on three ideas.

Light first

Floor-to-ceiling glass on every residence, corner layouts wherever the structure allows, and ceiling heights that let the city in. The architecture is engineered around daylight, not around the floor plate.

Honest materials

Exposed concrete, blackened steel, white oak, and stone — left to read as themselves. Nothing faux, nothing fighting for attention. The loft language carries through from the lobby to the penthouse.

Service that disappears

A 24/7 team, seamless arrival, and amenities you actually use. Good service should be felt, not noticed — the building handles the logistics so the city stays the focus.

Of the neighborhood

A ground floor that gives back to the street: retail, a café, and a public plaza. LOFT52 is designed to make Central Avenue better, not just taller.

The neighborhood

Central Avenue & the arts district.

Step out the lobby and you're in it — the Dali Museum, the new St. Pete Pier, the Saturday Morning Market, and a stretch of independent restaurants and galleries that made the city a destination.

Walk everywhere

Waterfront parks, the pier, and the EDGE District are all under a 12-minute walk. A 96 Walk Score means most days the car never leaves the garage.

Connected out

Quick access to I-275 puts Tampa International Airport at roughly 30 minutes and the Gulf beaches at 20 — close to the energy, close to the escape.

From groundbreaking to move-in

The timeline.

2024

Land assembly & design

Site acquired on Central Avenue. Schematic design completed with the architecture team and entitlements secured with the City of St. Petersburg.

2025

Sales gallery opens

Priority list opens to the public and the first release of residences goes to contract. The on-site sales gallery and model loft open by appointment.

2026

Vertical construction

The tower rises floor by floor through the year, with structure topping out and the amenity floors taking shape ahead of finishing.

2027

Move-in & closings

Final finishes, building inspections, and the first residents take occupancy. Closings begin and the amenity floors open.
